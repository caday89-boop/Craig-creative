My methodology

Active feedback loops, not finished-product reveals.

Most software projects fail at the same point — the team building the tool never actually sat with the people who'd use it. They built from a spec, not from observation. The result is software that technically works but nobody wants to use.

My delivery method puts end users at the centre of every iteration. Working sessions with the people whose work the tool will change. Demos every sprint. We don't ship features — we ship what your team actually needs. That's the discipline I learned shipping at enterprise scale, and it's what I bring to every SMB engagement.

How it works

A cycle, not a
handoff.

From first conversation to handover — every phase is collaborative. You're never waiting months to find out if we're on track, and you're never handed a deliverable you didn't help shape.

01
Listen first
A 30-minute conversation about what you're trying to fix and the people whose work it'll change. No pitch. No pressure. Just understanding.
02
Co-design the scope
A clear proposal: what we'll build together, when, for how much. Built with input from the people who'll actually use the tool — not just whoever signs the contract.
03
Build & iterate
Working demos every sprint, real feedback every week. Every iteration responds to your team's voice. Course correction is built in.
04
Hand it back to you
Full docs, team walkthrough, warranty period. The goal is that your team owns and runs it independently — not that you depend on me forever.
↻
The whole project is a feedback loop, not a waterfall.
You see real progress every week — not just at the end. Your team's input shapes every iteration. And by the time we hand it over, the tool already feels like theirs because they helped build it.
